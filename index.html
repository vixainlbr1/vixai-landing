The problem

Most companies do not lack technical data. They lack technical memory. Inspection photos are scattered. Measurements live in spreadsheets. Reports are stored as PDFs. Decisions exist only in emails or in people’s heads.

When experienced engineers leave, the knowledge leaves with them. The same technical problems are analyzed repeatedly, past decisions are forgotten, and risk slowly accumulates.

Why traditional asset management fails

  • CMMS systems track tasks, not reasoning
  • Document repositories store files, not context
  • Spreadsheets cannot represent technical history
  • Photos without structure are not evidence

Technical history is not a folder. It is a timeline of decisions.

The Vixai concept

Vixai is built around one core idea:

Every physical asset has a technical history.

That history includes inspections, images, measurements, failures, maintenance actions and — most importantly — the decisions taken and why they were taken.

Vixai structures this history into a living technical memory that can be queried, compared and reused across assets and teams.

What “technical memory” means

  • Evidence: images, readings, reports and manuals
  • Context: what changed, when, and under which operating conditions
  • Decisions: the “why”, alternatives considered, risk accepted
  • Outcomes: what happened after — so teams can learn, not guess

The cost isn’t losing data. It’s losing the “why”.

Where Vixai goes next

The roadmap expands the same core idea — technical memory — into system-level context, alerts, and richer signals.

Manual learning (docs → knowledge)

Upload equipment manuals so teams can ask: limits, procedures, troubleshooting — with source references.

Asset connections (series / parallel)

Connect assets into real operating flows to understand dependencies, redundancy, and impact across the system.

Alerts with context

Maintenance windows, preventive reviews, and “decision review” alerts — based on evidence and history.

Multimodal signals

Add vibration/noise, thermal (thermography), and chemical analysis as technical events to enrich memory.

Failure analysis

Capture failure modes, symptoms, evidence, root-cause (when known) and link outcomes back to decisions.

Maintenance modules (P/C/PdM)

Preventive, corrective and predictive maintenance as part of memory — without becoming a heavy workflow tool.

The long-term goal: technical memory that spans assets + systems + signals + decisions, so teams learn from real history — not tribal knowledge.
