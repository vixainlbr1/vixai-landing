The problem

Most companies do not lack technical data. They lack technical memory. Inspection photos are scattered. Measurements live in spreadsheets. Reports are stored as PDFs. Decisions exist only in emails or in people’s heads.

When experienced engineers leave, the knowledge leaves with them. The same technical problems are analyzed repeatedly, past decisions are forgotten, and risk slowly accumulates.

Why traditional asset management fails

  • CMMS systems track tasks, not reasoning
  • Document repositories store files, not context
  • Spreadsheets cannot represent technical history
  • Photos without structure are not evidence

Technical history is not a folder. It is a timeline of decisions.

The Vixai concept

Vixai is built around one core idea:

Every physical asset has a technical history.

That history includes inspections, images, measurements, failures, maintenance actions and — most importantly — the decisions taken and why they were taken.

Vixai structures this history into a living technical memory that can be queried, compared and reused.

How it works

Core workflow

  • Assets are uniquely identified and tracked over time
  • Every inspection, measurement or image becomes a technical event
  • Events are linked to evidence and decisions
  • History becomes searchable and comparable
  • AI assists by summarizing, finding similar cases and highlighting patterns

Principle

AI does not replace engineers. It supports engineering judgment by keeping context intact, surfacing evidence quickly, and reducing repeat analysis.

Typical queries

  • • “What happened last time we saw this defect mode?”
  • • “Show similar assets and the decision outcomes.”
  • • “Summarize inspection evidence and rationale in one page.”
