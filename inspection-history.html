Inspection History

Inspections are performed to reduce uncertainty. However, when inspection results are treated as isolated reports, their long-term value is lost.

What inspection history really means

Inspection history is the structured record of observations, images, measurements and findings collected over time — linked to the decisions taken as a result of those inspections.

A single inspection answers the question: What is the condition now?
Inspection history answers: How did we get here, and why?

The problem with inspection reports

Most inspection data is stored as static reports. Once filed, the reasoning behind the conclusions quickly fades.

When similar issues appear years later, engineers must re-evaluate the same conditions without access to past context, assumptions or outcomes.

Images without history are not evidence

Inspection photos are often stored without structure or comparison. Without historical reference, images cannot reliably support engineering decisions or risk assessments.

Inspection history transforms images from documentation into evidence.

Inspection history as a decision tool

When inspection data is preserved as history, organizations can:

  • Compare current findings with past conditions
  • Understand why previous decisions were taken
  • Identify recurring patterns and degradation trends
  • Reduce repeated analysis and uncertainty

Inspection history supports engineering judgment instead of replacing it.
